Research Papers

Copy Less, Ground More: Overcoming Repetitive Copying in Long-Context Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Reinforcement LearningarXiv

Researchers have identified a failure mode they term "repetitive copying" in large language models performing step-by-step reasoning over long contexts, where models reproduce large portions of input text in their reasoning traces rather than making analytical progress toward a solution. The paper proposes an evidence-aware reinforcement learning approach to reduce this behavior by rewarding models for grounding their reasoning in relevant evidence rather than copying. The finding is practically significant because long-context reasoning is an increasingly important capability, and copying behavior wastes context window space while degrading output quality.

Appearance Pointers -- Multimodal Region Control of Diffusion TransformersarXiv

Researchers have proposed Appearance Pointers, a method for achieving fine-grained regional control over image generation using Diffusion Transformers (DiTs). The work addresses a persistent limitation in controllable image generation: text prompts alone cannot reliably specify materials, object identities, or spatial arrangements at the region level. The approach leverages DiTs' native ability to process heterogeneous token types — both text and image — and introduces mechanisms to specify where and how visual appearance from reference images should be applied within generated outputs. This kind of multimodal regional control is relevant to creative professionals who require precise compositional guidance beyond what natural language can express.
