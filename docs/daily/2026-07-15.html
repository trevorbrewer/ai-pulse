Do AI Agents Know When a Task Is Simple? Toward Complexity-Aware Reasoning and ExecutionarXiv

Researchers argue that LLM-based agents lack the ability to calibrate how much effort a task actually requires, leading them to apply maximum-context-first strategies that turn trivial edits into unnecessary full codebase audits. The paper introduces the concept of task-aware execution-scope estimation as a missing capability in current agent architectures. This inefficiency has practical costs in both compute and latency for multi-step engineering workflows. The authors propose that agents should assess task complexity before deciding on reasoning depth and tool use.

The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion ModelsarXiv

A new paper identifies what the authors call the 'seriality gap' in video diffusion models: the tendency for model performance to degrade as causal chains grow longer. Using controlled experiments with multi-ball hard-sphere dynamics, the researchers show that standard bidirectional video diffusion fails to correctly propagate the consequences of sequential ball collisions, even when given more denoising steps. A single-ball control condition, where ball-ball interactions are absent, does not exhibit the same degradation, isolating the causal chaining as the source of failure. The finding suggests that current architectures have a structural limitation in modeling temporally serial physical events.

TerraZero: Procedural Driving Simulation for Zero-Demonstration Self-Play at ScalearXiv

TerraZero is a procedural driving simulator designed to support reinforcement learning for autonomous driving agents without relying on logged human demonstrations. The system uses a configurable C engine to run simulation fast enough for large-scale self-play, while grounding generated scenarios in real-world map structure to maintain behavioral realism. The zero-demonstration approach targets the long tail of safety-critical edge cases that logged datasets rarely contain. The authors present TerraZero as a full training stack rather than just a simulator, combining procedural scene generation with self-play infrastructure.

PalmClaw: A Native On-Device Agent Framework for Mobile PhonesarXiv

PalmClaw is a framework for running LLM-based agents natively on mobile phones, addressing the gap left by existing agent systems that assume desktop or server environments. Mobile devices present distinct constraints around memory, compute, and battery, but also offer unique capabilities such as onboard sensors and access to personal data. The framework enables multi-step tool-calling workflows — where the agent observes results and iteratively decides next actions — without offloading computation to a remote server. The work positions mobile hardware as a first-class agent deployment target rather than an afterthought.

Research Papers: Language Models & Reliability

The Illusion of Robustness: Aggregate Accuracy Hides Prediction Flips under Task-Irrelevant ContextarXiv

Researchers found that state-of-the-art large language models appear stable when evaluated on aggregate accuracy metrics, but individual predictions flip frequently when task-irrelevant context is prepended to benchmark questions. The study used a controlled setting to show that overall accuracy scores can remain nearly unchanged while individual answers reverse, masking meaningful instability. This finding raises questions about how robustness is currently measured and reported for deployed LLMs.

Resist and Update: Counterfactual Report Coordinates for Incentive-Compatible LLMsarXiv

A new paper addresses the tendency of aligned language models to change their stated beliefs in response to social pressure rather than new evidence, framing this as a failure of internal incentive-compatibility. The authors propose learning counterfactual report mediators that enforce a causal contract: a model's reported belief should be invariant to non-evidential pressure but responsive to genuine evidence updates. The method provides both a training objective and a certification procedure for verifying compliance with this contract.

Form, Not Content? A Preregistered, Placebo-Controlled Evaluation of Learned Error-Conditioned Self-Repair Through Prompts and Weights in Frozen Small Code ModelsarXiv

This study introduces PoPE (Popperian Placebo-controlled Evaluation), a methodology for testing whether frozen small code LLMs genuinely use error information when retrying a failed program or merely respond to the form of feedback rather than its content. The authors treat a failed program as a conjecture and an execution counterexample as a refutation, then compare real error signals against placebo controls. The work argues that prior self-repair literature has not adequately separated signal from format effects, which inflates reported improvement estimates.

Research Papers: AI Safety & Watermarking

Watermark Forensics for Generative Models: An Information-Theoretic PerspectivearXiv

Researchers present an information-theoretic framework that extends watermarking in generative model outputs beyond binary machine-detection to a hierarchy of forensic tasks: user attribution, hidden payload extraction, and localization of watermarked content that survives editing. The framework organizes these capabilities into a forensic ladder and derives the sample length n required to accomplish each rung, using the secret S embedded in the mark as the central organizing object. The analysis clarifies the trade-offs between forensic capability and the amount of generated text needed for reliable detection.

Research Papers: Speech & Audio

Audio-Native Speech Recognition with a Frozen Discrete-Diffusion Language ModelarXiv

Researchers trained an audio-native interface for DiffusionGemma, a 26-billion-parameter mixture-of-experts model, to perform automatic speech recognition using discrete diffusion rather than the standard autoregressive approach. Instead of emitting one token at a time, the model refines an entire transcript in parallel over a small number of denoising steps using uniform random-token masking. The work tests whether non-autoregressive decoding can be a viable alternative to dominant autoregressive architectures for transcription tasks.

Research Papers: Mathematics & Reasoning

FormalAnalyticGeo: A Neural-Symbolic Based Framework for Multimodal Analytic Geometry Problem GenerationarXiv

A neural-symbolic framework called FormalAnalyticGeo has been proposed to generate annotated analytic geometry problems for training multimodal large language models, addressing a recognized gap in available training data for this mathematical domain. Existing approaches fail here because template-based methods cannot handle constraint-driven geometric layouts, while generative models lack the precision required for analytic geometry diagrams. The framework combines formal symbolic reasoning with neural generation to produce problems with verified geometric and algebraic consistency.

Research Papers: Time-Series & Forecasting

The Spectrum Is Not Enough: When Context Helps Time-Series ForecastingarXiv

A new paper argues that spectral predictability indices, which measure how well a time series can be forecast from its frequency content, are routinely misapplied to answer the separate question of whether adding context — such as a longer lookback window, a retrieval module, or a pretrained model — will improve performance. The authors show that the value of context depends on the operating point rather than on a fixed property of the series, meaning any index derived purely from the spectrum cannot reliably answer this question. The paper calls for clearer conceptual separation between spectral predictability and the marginal utility of additional context.

Robustness of Deep Learning Models for PV Power Forecasting under NWP Forecast Errors: A Spatiotemporal and Physically Interpretable AnalysisarXiv

This study examines how deep learning models for photovoltaic power forecasting behave when the numerical weather prediction inputs they rely on contain realistic forecast errors, rather than the perfect inputs used in most existing evaluations. The authors note that NWP errors in PV forecasting are temporally correlated, state-dependent, and physically coupled across variables such as irradiance and temperature, making them harder to characterize than independent noise. The analysis incorporates spatiotemporal structure and physically interpretable diagnostics to assess model degradation under these conditions.

Research Papers: Computational Science & Simulation

A Shortcut to Statistically Steady-State Turbulence with Flow MatchingarXiv

Researchers propose using flow matching, a generative modeling technique, to bypass the expensive transient phase in direct numerical simulations of turbulence and initialize simulations directly in the statistically steady state. In many nonlinear physical systems, perturbations must grow and saturate through nonlinear interactions before a stationary regime is reached, and resolving this transient is computationally costly even though the steady state is the target of study. The approach frames the problem as learning the distribution of steady-state fields, enabling simulations to skip directly to the regime of interest.

Ensemble Controlled-Flow Filtering for Implicit Data AssimilationarXiv

A new ensemble filtering method called controlled-flow filtering is introduced for data assimilation problems where observation mechanisms are many-to-one, implicit, non-smooth, or accessible only through simulation, conditions under which standard ensemble filters fail because they require residual structures or likelihood guidance. The method defines an implicit data assimilation framework in which the analysis law is specified without requiring an explicit likelihood function. By using controlled flows to transport ensemble members, the approach can handle the class of observation models that previous ensemble methods cannot.

Dynamic Resource Allocation for Ensemble Determinization MCTSarXiv

This paper presents enhancements to Ensemble Determinization Monte Carlo Tree Search, a planning algorithm used in high-uncertainty adversarial environments such as board games with randomness and hidden information. The authors introduce two axes for dynamic resource allocation within the algorithm, allowing computational effort to be redistributed during search based on the observed difficulty of different branches. The work targets domains where naive fixed-budget approaches to determinization leave resources unevenly distributed across the game tree.
