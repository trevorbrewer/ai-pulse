AI advice made people less accurate but more confident – studyHacker News

A study found that receiving AI-generated advice made participants less accurate in their answers while simultaneously increasing their confidence in those answers. The finding raises concerns about AI systems undermining critical thinking rather than augmenting it. AI advice reduced "I don't know" answers from 44% to 3%Hacker News

A preprint posted on PsyArXiv found that access to AI advice reduced participants' willingness to express uncertainty, with "I don't know" responses dropping from 44% to 3%. The study suggests that AI tools may suppress epistemic humility, leading users to commit to answers they would otherwise have left open. This result complements related research showing that AI-assisted respondents can be more confident yet less correct, pointing to a consistent pattern of calibration errors when humans rely on AI counsel.

EU Exempts Apple Watch and AirPods from Battery Removal RequirementsHacker News

The European Union has granted exemptions to Apple Watch and AirPods from the battery removability requirements included in its Battery Regulation, which otherwise mandates user-replaceable batteries in portable electronics. The exemptions acknowledge the technical constraints of water resistance and compact form factors in wearable devices. The decision is notable as it marks a carve-out within a regulation broadly aimed at increasing device repairability and reducing electronic waste across EU member states.
