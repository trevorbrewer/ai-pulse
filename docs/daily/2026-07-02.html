Is One Layer Enough? Training A Single Transformer Layer Can Match Full-Parameter RL TrainingarXiv

Researchers have published findings challenging the standard practice of updating all parameters uniformly during reinforcement learning post-training of large language models. The study investigates how RL adaptation is distributed across transformer layers and finds that training only a single layer can match the performance of full-parameter RL training. This result questions the assumption that every layer contributes equally to the gains from RL fine-tuning, with practical implications for reducing the computational cost of post-training pipelines.
