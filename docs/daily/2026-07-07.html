A blog post from Vybe argues that developers over-rely on LLM inference for tasks that could be handled more cheaply and reliably with deterministic code. The post distinguishes between problems that genuinely require probabilistic language understanding and those — such as date formatting, validation, or lookup — where a conventional function produces more predictable results at a fraction of the cost. Research Papers

From Fixed to Free Cameras: Calibration-Free View-Robust Vision-Language-Action ModelarXiv

Researchers propose a Vision-Language-Action model for robotics that operates without requiring known camera extrinsics, addressing a key barrier to real-world deployment where cameras are frequently repositioned between training and inference. Existing view-robust VLA policies typically require explicit calibration data to tolerate camera movement, making them fragile in practice. The paper introduces a calibration-free approach that learns to handle arbitrary camera placements, which the authors argue is essential for scenarios where view robustness is most critical.

Weak-to-Strong Generalization via Direct On-Policy DistillationarXiv

This paper addresses the computational cost of applying reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards directly to large language models by proposing a weak-to-strong distillation alternative. The method runs RL on a smaller, cheaper model and then distills the resulting policy into a larger target model, avoiding the expensive rollout generation that makes on-policy RL on large models costly. The authors argue this approach could reduce post-training costs significantly as model sizes scale, and present results showing the distilled large model benefits from the smaller model's RL-trained reasoning improvements.

Interpretable Human-Label-Free Deep Learning for Real-Bogus Classification with Uncertainty QuantificationarXiv

Astronomers present a deep learning framework for classifying transient candidates in time-domain survey data as real astrophysical events or imaging artifacts, without requiring human-labeled training data. The system uses injected synthetic transients and artifact-dominated survey data to generate training labels, sidestepping the cost and noise of community annotation. Notably, the framework includes uncertainty quantification, allowing the pipeline to flag low-confidence classifications rather than silently producing unreliable outputs.

LLM-as-a-Verifier: A General-Purpose Verification FrameworkarXiv

Researchers introduce LLM-as-a-Verifier, a framework that treats verification — determining whether a proposed solution is correct — as a distinct and scalable capability for language models, separate from generation. The paper positions verification as a new axis along which LLM capability can be scaled, alongside pre-training and post-training compute. The framework provides fine-grained verification signals and is presented as general-purpose, applicable across domains rather than tied to a specific task type such as mathematical reasoning.

Search Beyond What Can Be Taught: Evolving the Knowledge Boundary in Agentic Visual GenerationarXiv

This paper tackles the problem of visual generators confidently producing incorrect or fabricated images when asked to render entities, events, or concepts that fall outside their training data. The authors frame this as a structural world-knowledge bottleneck and propose an agentic approach that incorporates search to retrieve up-to-date visual references at inference time. To support evaluation, they introduce SearchGen-20K and SearchGen-Bench, a dataset and benchmark containing 20,000 examples spanning new characters, trending entities, and post-cutoff events.

What Does a Discrete Diffusion Model Learn?arXiv

This theoretical paper examines what a discrete diffusion model is actually learning, arguing that apparent differences between a denoiser, a score ratio, and a bridge plug-in predictor are coordinate transformations of the same underlying object at the level of jump rates in a continuous-time Markov chain. The authors show that training and sampling a neural network in the wrong coordinate system changes the effective process being learned, which can silently corrupt model behavior. The work provides a rigorous derivation of the CTMC ELBO for arbitrary noising processes, including careful treatment of boundary terms.

TabPack: Efficient Hyperparameter Ensembles for Tabular Deep LearningarXiv

Researchers introduce TabPack, an ensemble method for tabular deep learning that combines multiple multilayer perceptrons with varying hyperparameter configurations rather than using a single shared set across all ensemble members. Existing efficient MLP ensemble methods for tabular data use uniform hyperparameters, which requires separate tuning to reach peak performance. TabPack is designed for strong out-of-the-box performance without per-dataset hyperparameter search, addressing a practical limitation for practitioners working across diverse tabular datasets.

CompactionRL: Reinforcement Learning with Context Compaction for Long-Horizon AgentsarXiv

Researchers have proposed CompactionRL, a framework that integrates context compaction into reinforcement learning to enable LLM-based agents to operate beyond their fixed context window limits. Long-horizon agentic tasks are often cut short when interaction trajectories exceed maximum context length, and existing approaches to context summarization have not been systematically adapted for RL training. CompactionRL addresses this by summarizing prior interaction states into a compressed context and continuing rollouts from that compressed representation, allowing RL optimization to proceed across compaction boundaries. The work is notable because it formalizes a previously underexplored intersection between context management and policy learning for agents that must complete extended, multi-step tasks.
