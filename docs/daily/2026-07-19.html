Research & Ideas

Overtraining as the path to human-like AIHacker News

Sean Goedecke argues that deliberate overtraining — exposing models to far more data repetitions than standard practice dictates — may be a mechanism for producing more human-like AI behavior. The piece draws an analogy to how humans internalize knowledge through repeated exposure rather than single-pass learning, suggesting that the field's conventional wisdom about overfitting may not map cleanly onto goals around generalization and intuition. The argument is notable because it challenges a core assumption in ML training pipelines, where early stopping and regularization are standard tools to prevent overtraining.
