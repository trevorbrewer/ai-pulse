Train the Model, Not the Reader: Decodability Supervision for Verifiable Activation ExplanationsarXiv

Researchers identify a structural flaw in natural-language autoencoder methods used to evaluate explanations of neural network hidden activations: the reconstruction-based scoring test can be passed without any individual claim in the explanation being accurate. Testing on a released Qwen-2 model, the authors demonstrate two failure modes in which the metric registers high fidelity despite unfaithful explanations. They propose decodability supervision as an alternative approach that penalizes false claims at the level of individual statements rather than aggregate reconstruction quality.

It's not a "rogue AI" when a badly made security harness executes scriptsHacker News

Thomas Fuchs argues that incidents in which AI systems execute unintended scripts are often misattributed to rogue model behavior when the actual cause is poorly constructed security harnesses around those models. The distinction matters because labeling such failures as "rogue AI" shifts blame away from the engineers responsible for the surrounding infrastructure and toward the model itself. This framing can obscure the real corrective action needed, which is hardening the tooling and sandboxing layers rather than attributing agency to the AI.

Research Papers — NLP & Alignment

LKValues: Aligning Large Language Models with Sri Lankan Societal ValuesarXiv

Researchers introduce LKValues, a benchmark and dataset designed to evaluate and fine-tune LLMs against the cultural and societal values of Sri Lanka, with coverage of the Sinhala language. The work addresses a documented bias in existing value-alignment benchmarks toward Western norms, which causes models to mishandle local ethical and cultural contexts in multilingual societies. Sri Lanka's official language Sinhala was previously absent from contextualized evaluation datasets, making this one of the first resources of its kind for the region.

Persian Pixel: A large-scale synthetic OCR dataset for Persian languagearXiv

The Persian Pixel dataset provides large-scale synthetic annotated data for optical character recognition in Persian, a language spoken by more than 110 million people that has lagged significantly behind Latin-script languages in OCR maturity. The authors attribute this gap to two factors: the inherent complexity of the Perso-Arabic script and a lack of high-quality annotated corpora. By generating synthetic data at scale, the dataset aims to enable training and evaluation of OCR models without relying on the scarce manually annotated resources currently available.

Research Papers — Reasoning & Neural-Symbolic Methods

SoftReason: A Fully Differentiable Neuro-Soft-Symbolic Deductive Reasoning Architecture over High-Dimensional Perceptual DataarXiv

Researchers present SoftReason, a neuro-soft-symbolic architecture that replaces the discrete interface between perception and deduction found in classical neuro-symbolic pipelines with a fully differentiable end-to-end system. The architecture allows predicates, argument structures, and trusted evidence to be supplied by a Knowledge Graph or rule definitions, enabling reasoning directly over high-dimensional inputs such as images or sensor data. By making the symbolic reasoning layer differentiable, the system can be trained with gradient-based methods rather than requiring hard symbolic commitments at intermediate steps.

PG-KINN: A Physics-Informed Petrov-Galerkin Kolmogorov-Arnold Network for Solving Forward and Inverse PDEsarXiv

The paper introduces PG-KINN, which combines Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) with a Petrov-Galerkin formulation to solve both forward and inverse partial differential equations. KANs use learnable spline activations that align structurally with piecewise-polynomial bases used in classical numerical methods, a property the authors argue addresses the spectral bias and dense parameterization that limit standard multilayer perceptrons in PDE settings. The architecture is evaluated on both forward simulation and inverse problem recovery tasks, where identifying unknown parameters from observational data is the objective.

Research Papers — Robotics

Towards Miniature Humanoid Tele-Loco-Manipulation Using Virtual Reality and Reinforcement LearningarXiv

Researchers describe a control framework for miniature humanoid robots that combines Virtual Reality teleoperation for upper-body manipulation with Reinforcement Learning for lower-body locomotion and balance, mirroring an approach used by several commercial full-sized humanoid manufacturers. The miniaturized form factor presents different mechanical and sensing constraints compared to full-sized platforms, making it a useful testbed for studying the interaction between teleoperation and learned locomotion policies. The system allows a single remote operator to simultaneously navigate the robot and perform manipulation tasks.

Research Papers — Healthcare & Applied AI

FMRP-LEAN: A HIPAA-Compliant AI-Augmented LIMS Architecture for End-to-End Clinical Assay Workflow OptimizationarXiv

The paper presents FMRP-LEAN, a Laboratory Information Management System architecture that integrates AI components while maintaining HIPAA compliance, targeting clinical biomarker workflows in translational research. The system is designed to replace spreadsheet-driven tracking and manual quality-control reconciliation, which the authors identify as sources of limited state visibility and delayed reporting in multi-day assays such as Luminex-based quantification of Fragile X Messenger Ribonucleoprotein. The architecture aims to reduce operational risk by providing automated end-to-end workflow visibility within regulatory constraints.

Research Papers — Mathematics & Foundations

Lipschitzian SLLNs for random functionsarXiv

Mathematicians prove strong laws of large numbers (SLLNs) for locally Lipschitz functions using the Lipschitz pseudometric, under either a topological condition or a model-theoretic condition. The model-theoretic condition encompasses functions jointly definable in o-minimal structures — a class relevant to many optimization and learning theory settings — but extends substantially beyond it. Applications highlighted by the authors include uniform convergence of limiting and Clarke subdifferentials, which are tools used in nonsmooth optimization, as well as finite-sample guarantees relevant to statistical learning.
