Taken together, the coverage reveals a field actively negotiating the difference between AI as a capable collaborator and AI as a productivity multiplier.

Worth thinking about

If AI coding assistants make developers feel faster while objective measurements show them moving slower, what does that tell us about which metrics organizations should use to evaluate AI tool adoption — and are there plausible mechanisms by which the subjective and objective measures could both be correct simultaneously?

The 'gauge broke' study and the 3% time-savings research both found a disconnect between perceived and measured productivity, but organizations adopting AI tools typically rely on developer satisfaction surveys rather than controlled task timing. Understanding whether this gap reflects measurement error, genuinely different value dimensions, or a systematic bias in self-assessment matters for any team trying to make evidence-based decisions about AI tooling.
